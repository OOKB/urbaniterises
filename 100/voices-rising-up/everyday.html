Everyday

by Katie Lautar

Today I stand
with open arms
an open heart
and open sores

and I've almost
forgotten
what good
is for

because
beauty goes unnoticed

songs go unsung

and when our children
ask our dreams

we say we have none

oh i know it’s not like this
on both sides of the river
and there are places
in the world
people actually
deliver

and love is not a word
that they say
but it’s something
that they do
everyday

oh love is not a word
that they say
but it’s something
that they do
everyday

which is different than
rhetoric
or commentary

We need a movement
that gains momentum
the way water
gathers

or that spreads
the way
light
scatters

on both sides of the river
there are places in the world
people actually
deliver

and love is not a word
that they say
but it’s something
that they do
everyday

Go For It

I'm frighteningly human
I quiver
and I shake

and this ain't
gonna be
my last mistake

I don't know how long it will take you
to realize

that i won't bury the world's pain
in my eyes

and that's not something for which

I should apologize

but that
doubt
in your heart is

if i see things
that are broken
I mend them

and if I need
these walls
I'll defend them

if you have extra hands
well then
lend them

cuz the world needs
all the help
we can give

I'm not saying
we should work
til we're tireless

but I believe
we should lead lives
that inspire us

so that one day
the stars
might
admire us

as we shine
with the light
of our souls

so give it

with each and every
breath that you take

Katie Lautar

Katie Lautar was born and raised in Baltimore, where she has spent most of her life promoting social justice and social change for the general liberation and preservation of this beautiful world. Most of her early work was spent learning with and teaching the resilient inspiring fearless youth of Baltimore. She currently works in the forests and green spaces of Baltimore and is refining her skills as a painter, poet, and singer-songwriter.
