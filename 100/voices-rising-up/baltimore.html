Baltimore

by Mike Murphy

She lays for us at night like a battered lover
waiting for our return from the highway overpasses
from the ports
from the street corners
from the bus stops
of Sandtown and Pigtown and Highlandtown
waiting for a kiss, our sugar,
her bowed legs hidden below vacant storefront skirts
her pancake makeup hiding things best left unseen
words better left unsaid
waiting for us to love her again
like all the others do
no more, no less
the longshoremen poets
the cops and nickel baggers
the twelve o’clock boys
taken for rough rides down by the Patapsco
where the fish rot from the head
taking her for what she is
no more, no less
for what she gives
no more, no less
for what she remembers
and she remembers what she was
a charmed youth before the bruises
before the thankless lovers
she remembers
blue water lapping slowly at her eelgrass shores
she remembers
gentle lovers caressing her marble stoops
she remembers
what she once was in the glow
of a million soft blue lights
brilliant
once upon a time ago.

Mike Murphy

Mike Murphy lives and writes in Baltimore City with his wife Stacie and two cats named Daisy and Zelda. His work has appeared in Cobalt Review, Seltzer, Backbone Mountain Review, and Smile, Hon, You're in Baltimore, among others.

EndBud.
