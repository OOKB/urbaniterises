Ode to dead black Baltimore boys

by Chin-yer Wright

White life spans
divided by three
Marked for death with melanin
Your maleness decoded
as the sign of the beast
You are our most
unprotected
jewel
You are misunderstood
anger
You are the reasons
behind
The most powerless
And unanswered prayers
And meaningless marches
And justified chokeholds
And
We women selfishly attempt
To write poems
to ease our guilt

As if these words
Could ever resurrect you

I remember
asking my 17-year old
male student
where he saw himself in the next 5 years
And he replied
“dead”

I haven't seen him since
And I haven't even bothered to look for him

All I have is a black teen’s name
written on this disposable
dry erase board picket sign

It's not even dawn, I already see his name fading
And I
Know damn well
I better do something
More
than write this poem
something more than marching
something more than crying

Chin-yer Wright
